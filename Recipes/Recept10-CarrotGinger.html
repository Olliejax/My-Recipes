Carrot Ginger Juice

Fresh carrot juice with orange, lemon, and ginger. Bright, slightly sweet, and rich in carotenoids and vitamin C.

  • ⏲️ Prep time: 30 min
  • 🍳 Cook time: 0 min
  • 🍽️ Yield: 1 liter (1 bottle)

Ingredients

  • 8 large oranges
  • 8 large carrots
  • 1big chunck ginger
  • 5 large lemons, juiced

Directions

  1. Wash the carrots, oranges, lemon and ginger well.
  2. Cut everything into juicer-sized pieces.
  3. Run all through your juicer.
  4. Pour into a sterille bottle.
  5. Store in the fridge and shake before drinking.

Nutritional Benefits

Ingredient Vitamin/Compound Scientific Role Real Life Benefit
Carrot Beta-carotene (provitamin A) Converted to retinol, which supports phototransduction in the retina, epithelial integrity, and immune-cell differentiation. Supports eye health, skin health, and normal immune function.
Carrot Lutein and zeaxanthin Carotenoids that accumulate in ocular tissues and help quench reactive oxygen species generated by light exposure. Helps protect the eyes and may support long-term vision health.
Orange Vitamin C Acts as a reducing agent and enzyme cofactor in collagen synthesis, antioxidant recycling, and immune-cell protection. Supports immunity, skin structure, and recovery from daily oxidative stress.
Orange Folate Participates in one-carbon metabolism, nucleotide synthesis, and normal cell division. Supports healthy cell renewal and energy-related metabolic processes.
Lemon Vitamin C Contributes to antioxidant defense and collagen-related enzyme activity, and helps maintain connective tissue integrity. Helps keep skin, blood vessels, and connective tissues healthy.
Ginger Gingerols and shogaols Bioactive phenolics associated with modulation of inflammatory signaling pathways and gastrointestinal motility. May help digestion, reduce stomach discomfort, and support the body’s response to inflammation.
Carrot Potassium Supports membrane potential, neuromuscular signaling, and fluid balance through sodium-potassium transport systems. Helps with hydration balance, muscle function, and normal blood pressure.
