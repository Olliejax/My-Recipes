Thai‑Inspired Spice Paste

TitleThai‑Inspired Spice Paste
Tagspaste, vegan, asian
Date21-04-2026
Personal Score9 /10
AuthorMe ☺︎

A fragrant Thai‑style spice paste with coriander, cumin, fresh cilantro, chili peppers, shallot, galangal, soy sauce, turmeric, lime, and lemongrass.

  • ⏲️ Prep time: 10 min
  • 🍳 Cook time: 0 min (ready to use)
  • 🍽️ Servings: about 4 portions (paste base)

Ingredients

  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 2 handfuls fresh cilantro / coriander leaves
  • 4 fresh chili peppers (adjust to taste)
  • 2 shallots
  • 2 teaspoons galangal / Thai ginger, finely grated
  • 2 teaspoons soy sauce
  • 2 teaspoons turmeric powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Juice and zest of 1 lime
  • 3 stalks lemongrass, white part only, finely chopped
  • 1–2 tablespoons water or oil (optional, for desired consistency)

Directions

  1. Wash and roughly chop the chili peppers, shallots, and lemongrass; remove the majority of the seeds if you prefer less heat.
  2. In a food processor or mortar and pestle, combine the chopped chili peppers, shallots, lemongrass, grated galangal, ground coriander, cumin, and turmeric.
  3. Blend or pound into a coarse paste, adding a small splash of water or a bit of oil if needed to help it come together.
  4. Add the soy sauce, lime juice, lime zest, and salt, then blend again until the mixture is smooth and the flavors are well integrated.
  5. Stir in the fresh cilantro and pulse briefly so the herbs are finely chopped but not totally destroyed.
  6. Taste and adjust seasoning: add more salt, soy sauce, turmeric, or chili according to your preference.
  7. Transfer the paste to a jar and store in the fridge for up to a week, or divide into portions and freeze for later use.
  8. Use as a base for curries, stir‑fries, noodle sauces, or marinades for tofu, tempeh, or vegetables.
