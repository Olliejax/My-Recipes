Kimchi Wontons

TitleKimchi Wontons

Crispy oven-baked wontons filled with Jerusalem artichoke-hazelnut puree and served with spicy kimchi dipping sauce.

  • ⏲️ Prep time: 30 min
  • 🍳 Cook time: 10 min
  • 🍽️ Servings: 12 wontons

Ingredients

For kimchi puree:
  • 1 garlic clove, finely chopped
  • 1 piece ginger (5g), finely chopped
  • 2 tbsp agave syrup
  • Sea salt
  • 2 tsp tamari (or soy sauce)
  • 1 spring onion stalk, finely chopped
  • ½ carrot, finely chopped
  • 2 tbsp gochugaru (Korean chili flakes)
  • 50 ml water
For wontons:
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 150 g Jerusalem artichoke, peeled and chopped
  • 25 g hazelnuts
  • 200 ml water
  • Sea salt
  • 12 wonton wrappers
To serve:
  • Fresh coriander

Directions

  1. Kimchi puree: Blend all kimchi puree ingredients until smooth. Use blender or stick blender.
  2. Wonton filling: Preheat oven to 180°C. Heat olive oil and cumin seeds in pot until seeds crackle.
  3. Add Jerusalem artichoke, hazelnuts, and water. Simmer until water evaporates and artichoke is soft.
  4. Puree with blender or stick blender. Season with salt.
  5. Assemble wontons: Line baking dish with parchment paper. Wet finger with water.
  6. Place 1 tsp filling in center of wonton wrapper. Wet all 4 corners, bring corners together, pinch to seal. Place in dish. Repeat.
  7. Bake 10 minutes until golden and crispy.
  8. Serve hot with kimchi puree for dipping and fresh coriander.

Variations

You can experiment with different fillings and flavors for the wontons.

  • → Spicy pumpkin wontons: Use pumpkin instead of Jerusalem artichoke, add chili flakes to filling.
  • → Mushroom wontons: Replace Jerusalem artichoke with finely chopped mushrooms.
  • → Sweet potato wontons: Use roasted sweet potato for naturally sweet filling.
