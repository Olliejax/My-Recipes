Spicy Sesame Garlic Stir‑Fry Sauce

Tagssauce, asian

A savory, slightly spicy sauce made with plant‑based oil, garlic, chili pepper, sesame oil, soy sauce, spring onion, and pear, great for stir‑fries and noodles.

  • ⏲️ Prep time: 5 min
  • 🍳 Cook time: 5 min
  • 🍽️ Servings: about 4 (sauce)

Ingredients

  • 3 tablespoons plant‑based oil (e.g. sunflower or rapeseed oil)
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 small fresh chili pepper, finely chopped (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 spring onions, thinly sliced
  • 1 small pear, grated or finely diced (optional, for subtle sweetness)
  • Pinch of salt if needed

Directions

  1. Heat the plant‑based oil in a small pan over medium heat.
  2. Add the minced garlic and chopped chili pepper and fry gently for about 1–2 minutes until fragrant, being careful not to burn the garlic.
  3. Remove the pan from the heat and stir in the sesame oil and soy sauce so the mixture doesn’t get too hot.
  4. Transfer the sauce to a bowl and let it cool slightly.
  5. Stir in the spring onions and the grated or diced pear; the pear adds a mild sweetness and helps the sauce cling to noodles or vegetables.
  6. Taste and adjust seasoning, adding a pinch of salt if needed.
  7. Drizzle over stir‑fried vegetables, noodles, or tofu, or use as a dipping sauce for dumplings or spring rolls.

Serving suggestions

  • Perfect tossed into a quick veggie stir‑fry or noodle dish.
  • Also great as a marinade base for tofu or tempeh before pan‑frying.
