Red Pepper Dip

TitleRed Pepper Dip
Tagsdip, snack, apero
Date2026-04-10
Personal Score9,5 /10
AuthorMe ☺︎

Ultra-creamy vegan red pepper dip made with roasted peppers, cashews, harissa aromatics, and bright lemon. Perfect with crackers, pita, or fresh vegetables.

  • ⏲️ Prep time: 20 min (+ 2h soaking)
  • 🍳 Cook time: 5 min
  • 🍽️ Servings: 6

Ingredients

  • ¼ cup + 1 tsp olive oil, divided
  • 1 medium shallot, chopped
  • 1 small hot chili, seeded if desired and chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, sliced
  • 1 tsp sweet paprika, plus extra for serving
  • 1 tsp nutritional yeast
  • 1 cup raw cashews, sliced almonds, sunflower seeds (or mix), soaked 2+ hours, drained
  • 1 roasted red pepper (jarred or homemade)
  • 2 tbsp water (plus extra if needed)
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 tsp white wine vinegar
  • 1 tsp Dijon mustard
  • 1 tsp coconut aminos (or ½ tsp tamari)
  • Sea salt and black pepper, to taste
  • Chopped herbs, for serving

Directions

  1. Heat 1 tsp olive oil in a small pan over medium heat. Sauté shallot, chili, and garlic until soft with slight browning, about 5 minutes.
  2. Add paprika and nutritional yeast, stir until fragrant (add splash of water if sticking). Remove from heat and cool slightly.
  3. In a blender, combine sautéed mixture, drained cashews/seeds, roasted pepper, water, lemon juice, tomato paste, vinegar, Dijon, coconut aminos, salt, pepper, and remaining ¼ cup olive oil.
  4. Blend on high 1-2 minutes until silky smooth, scraping sides as needed. Add water 1 tbsp at a time if too thick.
  5. Taste and adjust: more salt, lemon, spice, or nutritional yeast as desired. Blend again if adjusting.
  6. Serve with olive oil swirl, paprika sprinkle, and chopped herbs. Keeps 1 week in fridge.

Notes

  • → Use cashews + sunflower seeds for best texture, or all sunflower seeds for nut-free
  • → Fresno or cayenne peppers work great - adjust seeds for heat level
