Blueberry Muffins

TitleBlueberry Muffins
Tagsbreakfast, snack
Date21-04-2026
Personal Score9 /10
AuthorMe ☺︎

Moist, lightly sweet muffins made with chia seeds, self‑raising flour, banana, almonds, olive oil, maple syrup, apple, and blueberries.

  • ⏲️ Prep time: 10 min
  • 🍳 Cook time: 25 min
  • 🍽️ Servings: 8–10 muffins

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon chia seeds + 2 tablespoons water (chia “egg”)
  • 150 g self‑rising flour
  • 60 g chopped almonds
  • 1 large ripe banana, mashed
  • 80 ml olive oil
  • 2–3 tablespoons maple syrup or agave (adjust to taste)
  • 1 apple, peeled and grated or finely diced
  • 100 g fresh or frozen blueberries
  • Pinch of salt

Directions

  1. Preheat the oven to 180°C (350°F) and line a muffin tin with paper liners.
  2. Prepare the chia “egg”: mix 1 tablespoon chia seeds with 2 tablespoons water and let sit for 5–10 minutes until thick and gel‑like.
  3. In a large bowl, combine the mashed banana, olive oil, maple syrup, and chia gel, then mix until smooth.
  4. Add the grated or diced apple and gently stir in.
  5. In a separate bowl, whisk together the self‑raising flour, chopped almonds, and a pinch of salt.
  6. Pour the dry ingredients into the wet mixture and stir just until combined; do not overmix.
  7. Gently fold in the blueberries so they don’t break too much.
  8. Divide the batter evenly among the muffin cups, filling each about two‑thirds full.
  9. Bake for 22–26 minutes, until the tops are golden and a toothpick comes out clean.
  10. Let cool in the tin for 5 minutes, then transfer to a wire rack to cool completely or enjoy warm.
